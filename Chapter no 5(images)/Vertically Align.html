align (additional Information)

The align attribute was commonly used to indicate how the other parts of a page should flow around an image. It has been removed from HTML5 and new websites should use CSS to control the alignment of images.

Aligning Images Vertically

There are three values that the align attribute can take that control how the image should align vertically with the text that surrounds it:

top

This aligns the first line of the surrounding text with the top of the image. My Name is Muhammad Waleed Khan & I Represent Pakistan. Pakistan Zindabad. The value of top places the first line of text near the top of the image and subsequent lines of text appear under the image.

middle or centre

This aligns the first line of the surrounding text with the middle of the image. My Name is Muhammad Waleed Khan & I Represent Pakistan. Pakistan Zindabad. The value of middle places the first line of text near the vertical middle of the image and subsequent lines of text appear under the image.

bottom

This aligns the first line of the surrounding text with the bottom of the image. My Name is Muhammad Waleed Khan & I Represent Pakistan. Pakistan Zindabad. The value of bottom places the first line of text near the bottom of the image and subsequent lines of text under the image.
